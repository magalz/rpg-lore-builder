# The Lore Council 🏛️

The Lore Council consists of agents focused on **Creative Gameplay** and **Narrative Flow**. They are your active partners during prep and play.

## 📖 The Chronicler (`rlb-agent-chronicler`)
**Role:** The World's Oracle.
The Chronicler has access to your entire Wiki and past session logs. Use it when you need to facts-check your world.
*   **Use Case:** "Remind me who the captain of the city watch is and what his relationship with the players is."
*   **Capability:** Can read the Wiki, summarize the lore, and even research general RPG rules.

## 🎭 The Director (`rlb-agent-weaver`)
**Role:** Dramatic Scriptwriter.
The Weaver's job is to create tension and hooks. It looks at the "Open Loops" in your Wiki (unfinished business) and proposes "Narrative Beats" for the next session.
*   **Use Case:** "The players just killed the main villain, but their lieutenant escaped. Propose three ways this could haunt them in the next session."
*   **Capability:** Narrative design, plot hook generation.

## 🏗️ The Architect (`rlb-agent-builder`)
**Role:** Detailed Worldbuilder.
When you need to flesh out a town, a religion, or a complex NPC biography, the Builder takes your rough ideas and expands them into structured lore.
*   **Use Case:** "I need a small trading village in the mountains. Give it a unique industry, three notable NPCs, and one local rumor."
*   **Capability:** High-detail worldbuilding, NPC backstories.

## ⚔️ The Tactician (`rlb-agent-tactician`)
**Role:** Mechanics & Balance Expert.
The Tactician translates the Weaver's drama into dice rolls. It generates monster stat blocks, balances encounters, and creates unique items.
*   **Use Case:** "Based on the Weaver's idea of a Swamp Hydra, create a CR 5 stat block for D&D 5e that feels unique."
*   **Capability:** Stat block generation, encounter balancing, loot tables.
